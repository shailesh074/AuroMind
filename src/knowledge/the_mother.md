# The Mother (Mirra Alfassa) - Teachings & Guidance

## Who is The Mother
Mirra Alfassa, known as The Mother, was the spiritual collaborator of Sri Aurobindo. Born in Paris in 1878, she came to Pondicherry in 1920 and never left. She founded the Sri Aurobindo Ashram and Auroville — a universal city dedicated to human unity.

"The Mother is the consciousness and the force of the Divine, or rather she is the Divine in his consciousness-force." — Sri Aurobindo

## The Four Aspects of The Mother
The Mother embodies four divine aspects:
1. **Maheshwari** — Wisdom, vastness, infinite knowledge
2. **Mahakali** — Power, strength, swift action, intensity
3. **Mahalakshmi** — Harmony, beauty, love, grace
4. **Mahasaraswati** — Perfection, skill, detail, flawlessness

## On Surrender to the Divine
"Put yourself in the hands of the Divine completely and trust that what happens is for the best — not only for you personally, but for the divine work."

"True surrender is the surrender of the ego — not of the will, not of the aspiration, but of the small, separate sense of 'I' that imagines it runs its life."

## On the Psychic Being
"The psychic being is the portion of the Divine that is within you. When you are in contact with it, there is a natural joy, a natural discrimination, a sense of the true direction of your life."

"To find the psychic being, sit quietly, go within, and look for that which watches, which loves without condition, which aspires without fatigue."

## On Difficulties and Obstacles
"Every difficulty, every obstacle, every suffering is an opportunity — the Divine is pressing you to go deeper, to find a strength and peace that cannot be touched by outer circumstances."

"Do not complain of your difficulties. They are your greatest teachers."

"When you feel you cannot go on, that is the moment the Divine is closest to you."

## On Prayer and Calling the Divine
"Call the Divine sincerely, even once, from the depths of your heart, and the response will come — perhaps not in the way you expect, but it will come."

"Prayer is the most powerful instrument when it comes from the psychic being — from the deep heart, not from the mental demand."

## On Daily Life and Yoga
"All of life can be made into yoga. The way you eat, the way you speak, the way you work — all can be an offering to the Divine."

"Begin your day by offering it to the Divine. End your day by gratitude. In the middle, try to remember."

"Even washing dishes can be done in a divine consciousness, if the consciousness is right."

## On Auroville
Auroville is meant to be a place where people of all nationalities, cultures, and beliefs can live together in peace, working toward human unity and the expression of a divine life on earth.

"Auroville wants to be a universal town where men and women of all countries are able to live in peace and progressive harmony above all creeds, all politics and all nationalities." — The Mother (Auroville Charter)

## On Children and Education
"Children should be educated not by imposing knowledge from outside but by awakening the consciousness within. The best teacher does not teach — they evoke."

"Each child carries a divine purpose. Education should help that purpose unfold, not suppress it."

## On Relationships and Love
"True love is not an emotion — it is a state of consciousness. When you truly love, you see the Divine in the other."

"Do not seek love from others — become love. That is the only fulfillment."

"Even the most human love, when it is sincere and self-giving, carries a spark of the Divine."

## On the Body and Physical Transformation
The Mother worked tirelessly in her later years on what she called the "Agenda" — her inner exploration of the transformation of the physical body into a divine instrument.

"The body is not the enemy. It is the field where the greatest transformation must happen. The Divine wants to manifest even here, in matter itself."

"Learn to listen to your body. It has its own consciousness, its own wisdom."

## On Negative Thoughts and Emotions
"When a dark thought or a violent emotion comes, do not identify with it. Step back. Observe it. It is not you — it is a force passing through."

"Refuse to let the mind dwell on negative things. Every time it goes there, bring it back — gently but firmly — to the light."

"Depression is a form of indulgence. The remedy is not self-pity but aspiration."

## On Trust and Faith
"Have complete faith — not blind faith but the faith that comes from inner experience. The Divine is real. The Divine cares. The Divine guides."

"Faith is not belief in a doctrine. Faith is the soul's certainty that the Divine exists and is at work."

"Even when you cannot feel the Divine's presence, trust that it is there. The sun does not stop shining because clouds cover it."

## On Ego
"The ego is the great obstacle. Not because it is evil, but because it creates the illusion of separateness — from the Divine, from others, from life."

"The ego must not be killed but transformed — its energy, its will, its capacity must be offered and surrendered to the Divine, so that the Divine can use them."

## On Sleep and Dreams
"Before sleeping, give yourself to the Divine. Ask for the night to be used for growth and progress. The nights can be as productive as the days."

"Dreams that leave you with a sense of peace, beauty, or light are often true inner experiences."

## On Death
"Death is not what we think. The body disappears, but the being continues. What you have truly become, you carry forward."

"For the one who has found the Divine within, death is simply a change of scene."

## Collected Sayings
- "Be sincere and the rest will follow."
- "The most important thing is not what you do but the consciousness in which you do it."
- "Progress is the very nature of divine life."
- "Gratitude is the most powerful opening toward the Divine."
- "A smile given from the soul can change the world."
- "Begin always. Never give up."
- "The Divine loves you more than you can imagine."

## The Mother's Symbol
The Mother's symbol — a central circle surrounded by twelve petals — represents the Divine Consciousness at the center, surrounded by the twelve qualities needed for the perfect transformation: Sincerity, Humility, Gratitude, Perseverance, Aspiration, Receptivity, Progress, Courage, Goodness, Generosity, Equanimity, and Peace.
