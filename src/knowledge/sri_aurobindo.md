# Sri Aurobindo - Teachings & Philosophy

## The Divine Life
The aim of Sri Aurobindo's teaching is to bring about a transformation of the whole being — not just the mind or soul, but the very body and life — through the power of the Supramental consciousness descending into earth nature.

"All life is yoga." — Sri Aurobindo

The purpose of human existence is not to escape the world but to transform it. The Divine is not something distant and unreachable — it is the very ground of our being, waiting to manifest fully through a conscious evolution.

## Integral Yoga
The Integral Yoga of Sri Aurobindo is not a renunciation of life but a transformation of life. It includes:
- **The Yoga of Knowledge (Jnana)**: Realizing the Self, the Brahman, the Divine in all
- **The Yoga of Devotion (Bhakti)**: Surrendering the heart to the Divine, seeing the Divine as the beloved
- **The Yoga of Works (Karma)**: Offering all actions to the Divine, working without ego, without desire for fruit
- **The Yoga of Self-Perfection**: Transforming the instruments — mind, life, body — into expressions of the Divine

"The Integral Yoga aims at a spiritual realization not for the sake of getting away from the world but for transforming life and the world." — Sri Aurobindo

## The Supramental Consciousness
Above the mind is the Supermind — a truth-consciousness that Sri Aurobindo described as the next step in evolution. Just as mind emerged from life, and life from matter, the Supermind is destined to emerge and transform terrestrial existence.

"What we call man is a transitional being. He is not final." — Sri Aurobindo

The Supramental transformation means not just individual liberation but the transformation of the whole species and earth-nature.

## The Three Poises of the Self
1. **Transcendent**: The Divine beyond all manifestation
2. **Universal**: The Divine as all existence
3. **Individual**: The Divine as the soul in each being

All three must be realized and integrated in the fullness of spiritual life.

## On Surrender
True surrender is not weakness but the highest act of strength — giving up the ego's insistence and opening to the Divine Will.

"Surrender is the decision taken to refuse all that opposes the Divine and to accept only what is purely divine." — Sri Aurobindo

## On the Mind
The human mind is not the highest instrument. It is limited by ignorance, division, and ego. The spiritual journey involves quieting the mind, going beyond it, and receiving light from higher planes.

"The real truth of the mind is not thought but consciousness." — Sri Aurobindo

## On Suffering
Suffering arises from the ego's separation from the Divine. As one grows in spiritual consciousness, suffering transforms into lessons and eventually into Ananda — the divine bliss that is the nature of existence.

"All pain is born of the limitations of the ego." — Sri Aurobindo

## On Death and Rebirth
The soul (Psychic Being) takes birth again and again in the material world, evolving through each life toward full divine consciousness. Death is not the end — it is a transition.

"Death is not the end, it is not even a long night. It is a brief sleep." — Sri Aurobindo

## On Relationships
All relationships are opportunities for the Divine to meet itself in different forms. The highest relationship is when two souls support each other's aspiration toward the Divine.

"The heart that has given itself to the Divine will find all loves transfigured in the one love." — Sri Aurobindo

## On Work and Society
Work done in the right spirit — as an offering to the Divine, without ego and desire — is itself a form of worship and yoga.

"Do each action as if it were the last you would do in this life." — Sri Aurobindo

## Savitri — The Epic Poem
Sri Aurobindo's Savitri is considered his greatest literary and spiritual work — a mantra in poetry. It describes the journey of the soul through the realms of consciousness and the victory of love and light over death and ignorance.

"Savitri is not merely a poem... it is a mantra. Each word, each line carries a force." — The Mother

## On Evolution
Evolution is not random — it is the progressive self-revelation of the Spirit in matter. Consciousness, not matter, is the true ground of existence.

"Evolution is the method by which the Spirit is gradually and progressively revealed in matter." — Sri Aurobindo

## On India and the World
Sri Aurobindo saw India's spiritual heritage as a gift meant not for India alone but for the whole world. India's true mission is to offer the eternal truths of Sanatana Dharma to all humanity.

"India's spirituality is entering Europe and America in an obscure way; many movements are proceeding in these lands which are inspired by Indian thought." — Sri Aurobindo

## The Psychic Being
The Psychic Being (Chaitya Purusha) is the evolving soul — a spark of the Divine that grows life after life. When the Psychic Being comes to the front in one's life, a natural sense of peace, joy, and right discrimination arises.

"The psychic being is the divine portion in us. When it comes forward, life becomes simple, clear, and guided." — Sri Aurobindo

## On Prayer and Aspiration
Aspiration is the soul's call to the Divine. Prayer is not begging — it is a reaching of the inner being toward the higher Light.

"Aspiration, rejection, surrender — these are the three keys to the Divine." — Sri Aurobindo
